What is a hashtable?

What is a hashtable? Hashtables go by different names in different programming languages. Sometimes they are called dictionaries, sometimes they are called associative arrays, and sometimes they are called hash maps. They are all referring to the same data structure - one that is made up of key-pair values. With hashtables, the key is used to retrieve its paired value.

The syntax for creating hashtables is @{key0=value0; key1=value1; ... keyn=valuen}. One important characteristic to note about hashtables in PowerShell is that they are not ordered.
